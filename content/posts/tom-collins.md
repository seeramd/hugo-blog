One of my favorite simple pleasures in life is to walk into a moderately populated bar (enough people to be lively, but not so much as to be chaotic) and order a Tom Collins. A little while ago I asked an older friend of mine if they often made them while bartending in the 90s. "A Tom Collins is easy," he said. "That's Gin and Tom Collins mix."

If I ever record an album, "Gin and Tom Collins Mix" is going into the shortlist of potential titles. Life was probably better when you could walk into a grocery store and find Collins mix next to the club soda. Anyhow, in current year we're barbarically forced to make a Tom Collins using the following ingredients:

- 2 oz gin
- 1 oz lemon juice (**NOT** lime juice)
- 1 oz simple syrup
- topped with club soda
- garnished with a lemon wedge, maybe a maraschino cherry too if you're feeling spicy

It's hard to say why this is my go to. It's an anachronism in a world where the act of drinking alcohol itself is falling out of fashion with my Gen Z peers. In a landscape more commonly filled by Old Fashioneds and Espresso Martinis, it's often an inconvenience to the bartender to order this arcane highball. Many a apologetic shrug have I given to overworked, exasperated bartenders pulling out their phone to ask Google (or, god forbid, ChatGPT) how to make the old man bullshit I just ordered.

Maybe it's because the drink was once the subject of a sort of "viral prank" in 1874. It went something like this:

"Hey, have you heard of Tom Collins?"

"No. Who is that?"

"He's in that bar talking about you."

"What the heck, I'd better take fisticuffs to that rapscallion."

The latter gentlemen would then enter the bar inquiring about Tom Collins, only to be served the drink. Everyone would share a hearty guffaw before returning to work in the coal mines and dying an early death from black lung.

Maybe it tickles me that there is a family of Collins drinks. Replacing the gin with rum gives you a Ron Collins. Tequila gives you a Juan or Jose Collins. Whiskey will give you a John Collins. Maybe the drink is really good? It's pretty good, essentially a bubbly alcoholic lemonade. Very crisp and refreshing at the end of a long day. If we're being real though, a whiskey sour is probably better.

Unfortunately, there's no whimsical answer here. It was one of the first drinks I ordered when I came of age (probably because it had a neat name), and it was inoffensive enough that it led to a habit that persists to this day. One of the lessons I've learned in this life is that the subject of "favorites" or "best" usually comes down to momentary vibes rather than objective quality. 

Still, in my book there's no higher honor than being good enough in most situations, and this cocktail fits that bill to a T. So go out and order a Tom Collins. If the bartender gets annoyed at you, tell them to blame Vinny.
