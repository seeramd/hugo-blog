Sometimes I wonder how much further ahead in life I'd be if I was a better hang. In the world of comedy, "the hang" is the fount from which all opportunity springs. Some may see a friend of an acquantaince's birthday party as nothing of particular note. Not worth the two hour commute, perhaps. No my friend, this is a marketing opportunity. For the low, low cost of ~~$2.75~~ $3, an extended evening in Bushwick, and pretending to be really into Big Thief ("God, Adrianne Lenker just sings directly into my soul"), you too can get a 5 minute guest spot in a bookstore cafe.

In the economy of time, "the hang" is the US dollar. Don't take my earlier scenario as (entirely) derision, it only makes sense if you think about it. A community is not a bank you can just withdraw from; you have to contribute something. No one owes you anything, and your time, your presence, is the very least you can give. It's unfortunate that this system is directly at odds with one of my favorite things in the world: not going to things.
